Musical Cubes — Museum Study Folio

Scope: This page documents the carved cube motifs on/near the choir arches of Rosslyn Chapel as a museum study. It distinguishes attested facts from interpretations. All photos and measurements must carry provenance and licenses. Schematic drawings below are exemplars only, not claims about specific cube numbers.

What is Attested

  • Rows of carved stone cubes with geometric relief motifs appear in/near the choir arches of Rosslyn Chapel.
  • The motifs include simple lines, crosses, circles, lattice‑like forms, and other repeating geometries.
  • Their **original function and meaning are not definitively established** in the historical record.

What is Hypothesized (label clearly)

  • Some researchers have proposed that certain motifs resemble cymatic (Chladni) figures—standing‑wave patterns made visible by vibrating plates.
  • On this basis, it has been suggested that a musical sequence could be inferred. These reconstructions vary and are contested.
  • Therefore, any mapping of cube → frequency → “score” presented here must be labeled **interpretation**, not established fact.

Editorial policy: On this page we present schematic exemplars only. Any photographic plate must include source, date, photographer, and license; any frequency mapping must carry a clear “Interpretive” label and a method note.

Method (How to Document Responsibly)

  1. Photograph or cite a cube motif with: location (arch/bay), angle, date, and license.
  2. Describe the motif in plain terms (e.g., “concentric double ring with central boss”).
  3. (Optional) Provide a schematic trace as SVG with the caption “schematic of observed motif.”
  4. (Optional, labeled) Offer a cymatic comparison (pattern family, not a single “exact” frequency) with a note like: “resembles nodal cross patterns in standing waves.”
  5. Store your entry in data/cubes.yml (see template below). Link the entry from a folio page when complete.
