Mystic Colors — Pigments & Spheres

Museum notes on medieval pigments + esoteric correspondences (creative overlay). Provenance varies by site; polychromy was common in medieval stonework.

Medieval Pigments

Ultramarine (Lapis) — Marian blue, heavens.
Vermilion (Cinnabar) — “blood” of life, seals.
Malachite — living green, vine/foliage.
Lead‑tin Yellow — haloes, light.
Gold leaf — glory, the sacred.

Note: Specific paint traces at Rosslyn are a matter for conservation records; this is an interpretive palette consistent with medieval practice.

Planet & Sefirot (working correspondences)

  • ☾ Yesod — silver, indigo, sea‑blue (Apprentice Pillar)
  • ♀ Netzach — green, rose (Lady Chapel)
  • ♄ Binah — black‑violet, leaden silver (Crypt)
  • ☉ Tiphereth — gold, amber (Choir/Nave harmonics)

These are **creative overlays** for learning/immersion; not claims about the chapel’s original paint scheme.
